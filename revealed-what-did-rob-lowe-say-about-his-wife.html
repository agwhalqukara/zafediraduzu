What Did Rob Lowe Say About His Wife?

I have always admired Rob Lowe as an actor and a person.

He has been in some of my favorite movies and shows, such as The Outsiders, St. Elmo’s Fire, The West Wing, and Parks and Recreation.

He is also a handsome and charming man, who seems to have a good sense of humor and a positive outlook on life.

But what I admire most about him is his love for his wife, Sheryl Berkoff, whom he has been married to for over 30 years.

Rob Lowe and Sheryl Berkoff have one of the most enduring and inspiring marriages in Hollywood.

They met on a blind date in 1983 but did not hit it off right away.

They reconnected six years later when Sheryl was working as a makeup artist on the set of Rob’s movie Bad Influence.

Rob Lowe and Sheryl Berkoff fell in love and tied the knot in 1991.

They have two sons, Matthew and John, who are now adults and pursuing their own careers.

Rob Lowe has never been shy about expressing his affection and admiration for his wife.

He often posts sweet and romantic messages on his social media accounts, praising Sheryl for her beauty, intelligence, talent, and support.

Rob Lowe also shares candid and adorable photos of them together, showing their chemistry and happiness.

He has also opened up about their relationship in interviews, revealing how they met, how they keep their marriage strong, and how they deal with the challenges and joys of life.

Here are some of the most touching and heartfelt things that Rob Lowe has said about his wife over the years.

– On their first meeting: “I met my wife on a blind date. It was the worst blind date in the history of blind dates. We went to a bar, and she was so bored with me that she left. Sheryl Berkoff said she had to go to the bathroom and never came back. I was crushed. I thought she was the most beautiful girl I had ever seen. But I guess she didn’t feel the same way about me”.

– On their second chance: “We met again six years later, on the set of Bad Influence. She was doing my makeup, and I recognized her right away. I said, ‘Hey, you’re the girl who ditched me on that blind date. ‘ She said, ‘Oh, yeah, I remember you. You were boring.’ I said, ‘Well, I’m not boring anymore. I’ve changed. Give me another chance.’ She said, ‘Okay, fine. Let’s go out.’ And that was it. We’ve been together ever since”.

– On their wedding day: “We got married on July 22, 1991, in a small and intimate ceremony at a hotel in Santa Barbara. It was the best day of my life. I was so nervous and excited. I couldn’t believe that I was marrying the woman of my dreams. She looked stunning in her white dress.  Sheryl Berkoff had a smile that lit up the room.  She was radiant. I still get goosebumps when I think about it”

– On their secret to a lasting marriage: “There is no secret. It’s just hard work, commitment, and love. We have our ups and downs, like any couple. We argue, we disagree, and we compromise. But we never give up on each other. We always try to communicate, listen, understand, respect, support, appreciate, surprise, to laugh, to have fun, to be romantic, to be faithful, to be loyal, to be honest, to be kind, to be generous, to be grateful, to be happy. We love each other more than anything in the world. That’s all there is to it”.
